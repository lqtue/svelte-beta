## SOP Outline

**900 words target** (buffer under 1,000-word limit). Each paragraph does exactly one job.

**Official framing to satisfy** (from 2024 open house — treat as the committee's checklist):
- Why you → Para 7 (access advantage, VMA, letter writers)
- Why now → Para 7 (VMA tractability argument) + Para 1 (climate urgency)
- Why Columbia → Para 6 (Bou Akar, Kurgan/C4SR, Slater — specific, not generic)
- Broad research themes/debates → Para 4 (spatial inequality + climate adaptation)
- Theoretical AND empirical interests → Para 2+3 (VMA = empirical; Bou Akar/Lefebvre = theoretical)
- Why does the field need your work → Para 4 + Para 7 close (spatial baseline gap + HCMC's under-studied status)
- What you've done + where heading → Para 2+3 (VMA + VnExpress) → dissertation arc

### Para 1 — The intellectual problem (hook)
Open with the Huynh (2015) gap — not by name, but by the problem he identified:

> "The most cited study of Ho Chi Minh City's planning history opens with a striking admission: despite documenting how planning failed the city across four decades, it lacks any spatial baseline for the period before 1975. The colonial grid, the wartime refugee settlements, the canals infilled to become boulevards — the spatial inheritance that shaped everything that followed — exist in historical accounts but not in a form that can be analyzed, measured, or overlaid against what came after."

Move fast. Do not over-explain.

### Para 2 — VMA as the response
Introduce VMA as research infrastructure (the Peter Bol / CHGIS model — not a startup, not a DH project). Include: first systematic georeferencing of French colonial and US Army cartographic sources for Saigon; knowledge graph linking property owners by ethnicity to georeferenced parcels; ~20 maps currently.

One sentence on origin: MPP → identified the spatial baseline gap → built the infrastructure to fill it.

### Para 3 — VnExpress as the complementary arc
Introduce the "50 năm quy hoạch" series as the post-1975 arc. Do not call it journalism. Frame as: systematic analysis of five decades of planning decisions, drawing on primary documentation and interviews with the officials who made them — name Võ Kim Cương (Former Deputy Chief Architect of HCMC).

Close the paragraph: the gap between this synthesis and rigorous spatial-historical scholarship is what the dissertation will fill.

### Para 4 — The research question
> "My dissertation asks: how have successive planning regimes — French colonial, wartime, socialist, reform-era — encoded spatial inequality into HCMC's built form, and what constraints does this accumulated urban inheritance impose on contemporary climate-adaptive planning decisions?"

State it flat. Do not hedge. Then one sentence: HCMC is one of the most climate-vulnerable megacities in the world and one of the least studied in terms of spatial planning history. That combination is the opportunity.

### Para 5 — Methods
Two sentences: spatial-historical analysis using VMA for the colonial and war-era periods; archival research, land use statistics, and semi-structured interviews for the post-1975 periods. VMA provides the spatial baseline from which each successive regime departed; traditional planning methods document how and why they departed.

Do not describe the technical pipeline in detail. "First systematic georeferencing" is sufficient.

### Para 6 — Why GSAPP specifically
Name faculty. Do not be generic.
- **Hiba Bou Akar**: Saigon post-1975 is a post-conflict city rebuilt under acute political contestation — her planning ethnography framework in *For the War Yet to Come* is the closest methodological precedent for this work.
- **Laura Kurgan + C4SR**: VMA's spatial research infrastructure fits the Conflict Urbanism series; no Southeast Asia project yet exists at C4SR. That gap is the opening.
- **Tom Slater**: Doi Moi land reform and the contemporary gentrification of Cholon is textbook Slater — the displacement framework applied to a Vietnamese transition economy.

One sentence: Columbia's GSAS structure gives simultaneous access to planning theory (Slater, Fainstein) and spatial humanities methods (C4SR) that no other program offers for this project.

### Para 7 — Why you, why now
Do not be modest. State the access advantage plainly:
- The only researcher with both the technical spatial infrastructure (VMA) and the established expert network (Võ Kim Cương, Nguyễn Đỗ Dũng, planning officials interviewed for VnExpress) to conduct this dissertation
- Letter writers Huynh The Du and Jonathan Pincus represent the leading Vietnam urban policy research circle; Huynh's published framework is the intellectual foundation the dissertation builds directly on
- PhD fieldwork interviews are pre-seeded — not contingent on cold outreach

Close with the timing argument: VMA is far enough along that the doctoral research is tractable now. Without it, this dissertation could not be written. With it, you are the only person positioned to write it.

## Writing Sample Plan

**Target**: 15–20 pages, double-spaced, academic format with footnotes

**Source material**:
- Article 2: "Hình hài Sài Gòn - TP HCM thay đổi thế nào sau 50 năm" — spatial changes, interactive maps, credited to Tuệ
- Article 4: "Thế giằng co định hình đô thị TP HCM" — power struggles, Võ Kim Cương: "the state's goals have lost to the market"

**Structure**:

| Section | Length | Content |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Abstract | 150 words | Argument, methods, findings |
| Introduction | 1.5pp | Research question, why HCMC, paper's contribution. Cite: Huynh 2015, Harvey 1973, Lefebvre 1991 |
| Theoretical framework | 2pp | Planning as facilitation device (Huynh); space as socially produced (Lefebvre); informality as a mode of planning (Roy 2009). One paragraph per concept. |
| Historical background | 1pp | Five-period arc. Cite: USAID 1972, Thrift & Forbes 1986 |
| Evidence: spatial form changes | 4pp | From Article 2. Add academic citations, VMA maps as data source, quantitative claims. At least one map figure. |
| Analysis: political economy of planning | 4pp | From Article 4. Võ Kim Cương quote read through Harvey's capital circulation lens + Gainsborough on party-state relations. |
| Discussion: climate adaptation | 2pp | Colonial canals infilled = why HCMC floods today. Connect to Downes et al. 2016 + IPCC path dependency concept. Dissertation's contemporary payoff in miniature. |
| Conclusion | 1pp | What rigorous spatial-historical analysis would add. The gap this paper gestures at but cannot fill — VMA's contribution. |
| References | — | 12–15 sources minimum |

**Tone**: Argument-driven, not descriptive. Writing for a planning committee, not a humanities journal.

## Why This Application Is Strong

**1. The gap argument is built in.**
Huynh (2015) is the established theoretical framework for HCMC planning — and he is your letter writer. Your dissertation fills the spatial baseline gap *he* explicitly identified. The intellectual continuity is verifiable, not claimed.

**2. Two complementary assets, neither alone sufficient.**
VMA covers the colonial and war-era periods that no other researcher has in spatial form. The VnExpress series covers the post-war arc with primary-source documentation no outsider could replicate. Together they span the full arc. Neither is the dissertation; both are the evidence base.

**3. Access advantage.**
Fieldwork is pre-seeded. Võ Kim Cương, Nguyễn Đỗ Dũng, and other planning officials interviewed for VnExpress are already in the network. This is not a contingency — it is an existing relationship.

**4. Faculty fit is specific.**
Bou Akar's post-conflict cities framework maps exactly onto HCMC 1975–1986. Slater's displacement framework maps exactly onto Doi Moi land reform. C4SR's Conflict Urbanism series has no Southeast Asia project. These are not generic claims about fit — they are structural matches.

**5. The timing argument is real.**
VMA's colonial spatial baseline now exists. This dissertation was not tractable before VMA. It is tractable now. You are the person who built the infrastructure and you are the only person positioned to use it for this specific question.
